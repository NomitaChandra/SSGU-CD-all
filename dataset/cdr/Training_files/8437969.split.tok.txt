Sinus arrest associated with continuous - infusion cimetidine .
The administration of intermittent intravenous infusions of cimetidine is infrequently associated with the development of bradyarrhythmias .
A 40 - year - old man with leukemia and no history of cardiac disease developed recurrent , brief episodes of apparent sinus arrest while receiving continuous - infusion cimetidine 50 mg / hour .
The arrhythmias were temporally related to cimetidine administration , disappeared after dechallenge , and did not recur during ranitidine treatment .
This is the first reported case of sinus arrest associated with continuous - infusion cimetidine .
