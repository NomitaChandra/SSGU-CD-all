Choreoathetoid movements associated with rapid adjustment to methadone .
Choreatiform hyperkinesias are known to be occasional movement abnormalities during intoxications with cocaine but not opiates .
This is a case report of euphoria and choreoathetoid movements both transiently induced by rapid adjustment to the selective mu - opioid receptor agonist methadone in an inpatient previously abusing heroine and cocaine .
In addition , minor EEG abnormalities occurred .
Possible underlying neurobiological phenomena are discussed .
