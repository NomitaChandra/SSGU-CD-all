Acute psychosis due to treatment with phenytoin in a nonepileptic patient .
The development of psychosis related to antiepileptic drug treatment is usually attributed to the interaction between the epileptic brain substratum and the antiepileptic drugs .
The case of a nonepileptic patient who developed psychosis following phenytoin treatment for trigeminal neuralgia is described .
This case suggests that the psychotic symptoms that occur following phenytoin treatment in some epileptic patients may be the direct result of medication , unrelated to seizures .
