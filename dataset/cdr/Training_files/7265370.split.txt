Triamterene nephrolithiasis complicating dyazide therapy.
A case of triamterene nephrolithiasis is reported in a man after 4 years of hydrochlorothiazide-triamterene therapy for hypertension.
The stone passed spontaneously and was found to contain a triamterene metabolite admixed with uric acid salts.
Factors affecting triamterene nephrolithiasis are discussed and 2 previously reported cases are reviewed.
