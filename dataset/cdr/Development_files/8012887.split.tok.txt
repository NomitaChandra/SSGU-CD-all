Failure of ancrod in the treatment of heparin - induced arterial thrombosis .
The morbidity and mortality associated with heparin - induced thrombosis remain high despite numerous empirical therapies .
Ancrod has been used successfully for prophylaxis against development of thrombosis in patients with heparin induced platelet aggregation who require brief reexposure to heparin , but its success in patients who have developed the thrombosis syndrome is not well defined .
The authors present a case of failure of ancrod treatment in a patient with heparin - induced thrombosis .
