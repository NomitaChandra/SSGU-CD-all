Hepatotoxicity of amiodarone .
Amiodarone has proved very effective in the treatment of otherwise resistant cardiac tachyarrhythmias .
The use of amiodarone has , however , been limited due to its serious side - effects .
A patient with cholestatic hepatitis due to amiodarone treatment is presented below and a review of the hepatotoxicity of amiodarone is given .
It is concluded that solid evidence exists of hepatic injury due to amiodarone treatment , including steatosis , alterations resembling alcoholic hepatitis , cholestatic hepatitis and micronodular cirrhosis of the liver .
Patients receiving amiodarone should be regularly screened with respect to hepatic enzyme levels .
Therapy should be discontinued on the suspicion of cholestatic injury or hepatomegaly .
