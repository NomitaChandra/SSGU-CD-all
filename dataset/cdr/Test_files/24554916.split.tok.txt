Cholestatic presentation of yellow phosphorus poisoning .
Yellow phosphorus , a component of certain pesticide pastes and fireworks , is well known to cause hepatotoxicity .
Poisoning with yellow phosphorus classically manifests with acute hepatitis leading to acute liver failure which may need liver transplantation .
We present a case of yellow phosphorus poisoning in which a patient presented with florid clinical features of cholestasis highlighting the fact that cholestasis can rarely be a presenting feature of yellow phosphorus hepatotoxicity .
